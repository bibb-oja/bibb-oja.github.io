# Online Job Advertisement Analysis

We are the OJA team at BIBB. We build algorithms and conduct research on German Online Job Advertisements to understand labor market dynamics, occupational requirements, and evolving skill demands.

### What we do

- Develop methods to analyze skill requirements in job markets
- Identify occupational trends and labor demand shifts
- Apply natural language processing to job posting text
- Connect research to economics and social science perspectives

### Why it matters

Job advertisements are a rich and timely data source for understanding how work is changing, which skills are most valued, and how employers signal labor market needs.

## Our Research Approach

At the core of our approach is the idea that complex job requirements should not be reduced to isolated, single-text spans, but rather understood as a network of interconnected components. Fuzzy concepts like skills and tasks naturally emerge when we break a requirement down into its atomic parts, such as actions, tools, and experience levels and explicitly map the typed relationships between them. 

By natively capturing these internal semantics, including critical contextual qualifiers like logical alternatives and urgency levels, our framework moves beyond basic keyword extraction to deliver the structural clarity required for deeper empirical labor market analysis and precise taxonomy alignment.
